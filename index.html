The art of book printing

Well, before i can tell you the juicy stories, i’ll first give some information on what book printing actually is, don’t worry i’ll keep it short and easy to understand!

Setting the type

First things first, setting the type. It all begins with selecting tiny metal letters, each one carefully chosen and arranged in reverse order. Line by painstaking line, these letters are assembled into a block of text, ready to tell a story or share a piece of knowledge. The process demands incredible focus and patience, as a single mistake—a letter placed in the wrong spot or upside down—can ruin an entire page.

The printing press

Once the type is set, the next step is to bring it to life with ink. We begin by coating the surface of the type with ink, applied using soft leather pads called ink balls. These pads are expertly dabbed and rolled across the tiny metal letters. It’s a delicate process, too much ink, and the letters will smudge, too little, and the impression will be faint. The ink itself is thick and rich, with a distinct smell that fills the air.

For a long time they believed the leather part was made of dogskin. This was never confirmed so believe what you want!

Printing is about so much more than just technique. It’s not just setting type, inking plates, and pulling levers—it’s about bringing ideas to life. It’s about stories, knowledge, and the way words can connect us all.

In our shop, we print books in Latin, Greek, Hebrew, and even more languages. It’s fascinating how words from so many places come together here, on our presses.
